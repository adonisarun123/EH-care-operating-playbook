2) Common Mistakes (What Kills Most Care Businesses)

‚ùå Mistake 1: Under-Promising Reliability

You say "we'll try to replace" but don't operationalize it. Clients churn after the first no-show.

Fix: Promise specific SLAs (e.g., "Replacement within 4 hours or next shift free") and build internal systems to deliver.

‚ùå Mistake 2: No Clear Scope = Disputes

Families expect house cleaning. Caregiver thinks it's only patient care. Conflict is inevitable.

Fix: Document scope in a "Care Plan" (see Section 7). Get sign-off before deployment.

‚ùå Mistake 3: No Middle Management

Founders try to scale by managing 100 caregivers directly. Burnout + chaos.

Fix: Hire supervisors at a 1:20 ratio (1 supervisor per 20 active caregivers). They handle audits, escalations, and morale.

‚ùå Mistake 4: Weak Verification

You skip police checks to move fast. One bad incident destroys your brand.

Fix: Minimum bar ‚Äî police verification, identity validation, reference checks. No shortcuts on trust.

4) Core Workflow: Lead ‚Üí Deployment ‚Üí Ongoing Care

End-to-End Process Flow

1. Inquiry Lead form / call
2. Assessment Needs call (30 min)
3. Care Plan Document scope
4. Matching Assign caregiver
5. Onboarding Intro + handoff
6. Ongoing Ops Daily logs + audits

Step-by-Step Breakdown

Step 1: Initial Inquiry & Qualification
  • Capture: Name, location, care type (elder/baby), package interest, urgency
  • Quick filter: Zone coverage check (don't take clients outside serviceable areas)
  • Assign to: Sales/CS rep (response within 2 hours)
Step 2: Needs Assessment Call (30 minutes)
  • For Elderly Care: Mobility, medical conditions, ADL level, medication needs, behavioral concerns
  • For Baby Care: Age, feeding schedule, sleep training, developmental milestones, special needs
  • Outcome: Recommended package + care plan draft
Step 3: Care Plan Documentation
  • Clearly define: What the caregiver will do vs. what they won't
  • Include: Schedule, responsibilities, red lines (e.g., no phone during baby care)
  • Get family sign-off (WhatsApp confirmation or e-sign)
Step 4: Caregiver Matching
  • Filter by: Skills, availability, zone, language, client preference
  • Share caregiver profile with family (photo, experience, verification status)
  • Finalize match within 24 hours of payment
Step 5: Deployment & First-Day Handoff
  • Supervisor accompanies caregiver on Day 1 (or video intro for remote)
  • Walk through care plan with family
  • First-hour check-in (supervisor calls family to confirm smooth start)
Step 6: Ongoing Operations
  • Daily attendance logs (check-in/check-out via app or WhatsApp)
  • Weekly supervisor audit (call or visit)
  • Monthly family feedback call
  • Renewal conversation at Day 25 of each cycle

5) Operations Layer: Who Does What

Team Structure (Phase 1: 0‚Äì50 active clients)

Founder / Operations Head

Owns: Client acquisition, pricing, supervisor hiring, escalation handling, weekly business review

Scheduler / Coordinator

Owns: Matching, shift scheduling, replacement tickets, attendance tracking

Supervisor (1 per 20 caregivers)

Owns: Weekly audits, first-day handoffs, complaint resolution, caregiver coaching

Trainer / Quality Lead

Owns: Caregiver onboarding, skill certification, retraining after incidents

Scaling Ratio: For every 20 active caregivers, hire 1 supervisor. For every 100 active clients, add 1 full-time scheduler.

Critical Operating Principles

  • Replacement is the hardest operation: Maintain a bench of 10‚Äì15% floaters to cover leaves, no-shows, emergencies
  • Documentation = accountability: No verbal promises. Everything goes into care plan, daily logs, or incident reports
  • Audits are non-negotiable: Weekly supervisor check-ins catch issues before they become complaints
  • Renewals are easier than acquisition: Track renewal dates; call clients 5 days before expiry

6) Quality Control: Hospital-Style Audits

Weekly Supervisor Audit (per caregiver)

Time: 10‚Äì15 minutes per caregiver. Must be logged in system/sheet.

Monthly Deep Audit (Random Sample)

  • In-person visit (10% of active caregivers/month)
  • Observe care delivery (with family consent)
  • Review daily logs for accuracy
  • Caregiver skill refresher if gaps found
Red Flag Protocol: If audit score < 3/5, immediate retraining. If second fail, remove from roster.

Incident Management Process

Report Via call/WhatsApp
Acknowledge Within 30 min
Investigate Call all parties
Resolve Action plan
Follow-up 24-hour check
Document Log outcome

Categories: Safety (priority 1), quality (priority 2), behavioral (priority 3). All P1 incidents require founder-level review.

9) Unit Economics: Make Payroll Sustainable

Pricing Must Include "Reliability Costs"

  • Caregiver salary / shift wages
  • Supervisor layer + audits
  • Verification + training cost amortization
  • Bench/floaters buffer
  • Customer success + support
  • Tech/CRM + communication costs
  • Margin (so you can scale)

Contribution Margin Framework (Simple)

Contribution Margin per Client (Monthly) =
  Revenue from Client
- Direct caregiver cost allocated
- Supervisor & audit allocation
- Replacement/bench allocation
- Variable ops costs (calls/whatsapp/transport)
= Contribution (‚Çπ)

Contribution % = Contribution / Revenue
Target gross margin guidance:
  • Elder care: 35‚Äì55% (depends on skill + hours)
  • Baby care: 30‚Äì50% (newborn can be premium)
Common trap: underpricing replacement readiness. If you promise reliability, you must price the bench into the package.

10) Trust & Safety: Your Brand Moat

Minimum Safety Standard (Before Deployment)

  • Police verification + identity validation
  • Address verification (caregiver)
  • Reference checks
  • Basic medical fitness check (where feasible)
  • Strict policy for baby care: no phone while holding baby, hygiene compliance
  • Clear written scope + boundaries + escalation
Zero ambiguity policy: If scope is unclear, disputes follow. Every client must have a signed/acknowledged scope document.
