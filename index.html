1) North Star: What You’re Building

Category Definition

Not staffing Not marketplace Not “maid on demand”

You are building Managed Care at Home: outcomes-led home-care for elderly and babies with structured SOPs, trained professionals, and supervision — where the company owns accountability end-to-end.

Your promise (non-negotiable):
  • Trust: verified caregivers + safety checks + clear boundaries
  • Reliability: attendance control + backup bench + replacement SLA
  • Quality: training + checklists + audits + incident system
  • Accountability: payroll model + supervisor ownership + escalation

How You Win vs. “Fast Service” Players

  • Shift fulfillment beats “fast booking” — families care about continuity.
  • Supervised care beats “just supply” — a care plan + audits changes outcomes.
  • Training & certification beats “any worker” — premium segments demand it.
  • Bench capacity beats “apologies” — you have replacement readiness.
What NOT to become: a generic domestic help agency with “elder care” and “baby care” labels. You must run this like a care operator (hospital-grade process, home-grade warmth).

3) Operating Model: Payroll-First Execution

Model A — Full Payroll (Maximum Control)

  • Caregivers are employees. You own training, discipline, attendance, replacements.
  • Best for: elderly, newborn, night shifts, repeat long-tenure clients.
  • Requires: strong utilization planning + bench budget.

Model B — Hybrid Payroll (Scale + Control)

  • Core payroll team: A-grade caregivers + supervisors + floaters.
  • Extended supply via partners ONLY after your verification + training + scoring.
  • Use to expand zones/cities without quality collapse.
Rule: Baby care + dementia care should be payroll-only in the early phase. This protects brand trust.

Bench & Utilization Rules (Make Payroll Profitable)

Target Utilization75–85% billable hours (payroll caregivers)
Bench / Floaters10–15% capacity reserved for replacements
Supervisor Ratio1 per 25–35 active caregivers
Complaint Rate< 2 per 100 shifts (target)

Payroll works when: pricing includes supervision + bench, and scheduling keeps utilization high without burning out caregivers.

6) SOP Library (Step-by-step)

SOP 1 — Lead Intake & Qualification (TAT: 10 minutes)
  • Capture: location, package needed, shift type (day/night/live-in), start date, language preference
  • Risk flags: newborn, dementia, post-surgery, aggressive behavior history, pets, smoking, cameras policy
  • Set expectations: verification, training-only caregivers, replacement SLA, trial rules
  • Next step: schedule assessment (phone/home) within 24 hours
Do not promise a caregiver without assessment + availability check. Promise the SLA, not a person.
SOP 2 — Assessment & Care Plan Creation (TAT: 24 hours)
  • Assessment checklist: patient/child routine, mobility level, meds schedule, allergies, food preferences, fall risk, emergency contacts
  • Home safety: bathroom grip support, sharp items, child-proofing, bed safety, lighting
  • Define boundaries: what caregiver can/can’t do; what family must provide (supplies, safe environment)
  • Escalation map: family → supervisor → ops head; emergency rules
  • Care plan output: one-page plan + daily log format + special notes
SOP 3 — Caregiver Matching & Deployment (TAT: 24–72 hours)
  • Match by: package fit, skill score, language, shift type tolerance, travel radius
  • Share 2–3 profiles maximum; include training score + verification status
  • Trial onboarding: introduce caregiver + show care plan + do home rules briefing
  • Confirm start: shift timing, salary components (if applicable), attendance rules
Golden rule: First day success decides renewal. Send supervisor visit/remote check-in within first 48 hours.
SOP 4 — Shift Start / Shift End Ritual (Daily)

Shift Start (first 5 minutes)

  • Confirm attendance via check-in (app/WhatsApp/IVR)
  • Review care plan highlights
  • Safety scan: baby sleep area / elder fall-risk spots
  • Ask family: any new instruction since last shift?

Shift End (last 5 minutes)

  • Update daily log (food, sleep, meds reminders, mood, activities)
  • Hand over key notes to family
  • Confirm next shift timing
  • Raise any concerns to supervisor immediately
SOP 5 — Attendance Control & Replacement SLA
  • Scheduler monitors shifts T-12 hours, T-2 hours, T+15 minutes no-show triggers
  • No-show rule: if caregiver misses check-in window → activate replacement ticket
  • Replacement order: floater → bench → partner pool (partner only if package allows)
  • Communicate to family: ETA + ID + supervisor contact
  • Close ticket: confirm arrival + first-hour check-in
SOP 6 — Incident Management (Response: within 30 minutes)
  • Classify: safety / medical emergency / misconduct / service mismatch / minor complaint
  • Immediate steps: ensure safety; inform family; escalate to supervisor & ops head
  • Document: time, what happened, witnesses, action taken, follow-up
  • Resolution: caregiver retraining / replacement / warning / termination
  • Post-incident review: SOP update + training loop
Non-negotiable: baby care incidents must be handled with a “zero-delay” escalation path.
SOP 7 — Supervisor Audit & Quality Checks
  • First-week audit for every new deployment
  • Thereafter: weekly/bi-weekly (based on package risk)
  • Audit covers: hygiene, care plan adherence, behavior, safety, family feedback
  • Score caregiver 1–5; <3 triggers retraining and closer supervision
SOP 8 — Training & Certification (Before deployment)
  • Core module: hygiene, communication, boundaries, attendance, escalation
  • Elder module: fall risk, mobility support, dementia basics, respectful care
  • Baby module: safe handling, sanitization, sleep safety basics, choking awareness basics
  • Certification requires: attendance + assessment + roleplay + supervisor sign-off
SOP 9 — Customer Success & Renewals
  • Day 1: welcome + what to expect + supervisor contact
  • Day 3: feedback check-in
  • Day 7: NPS + adjustments
  • Day 21: renewal discussion (if monthly)
  • Monthly: report summary + improvement suggestions

  • Quality: audits completed vs planned, incident review, retraining list

9) Unit Economics: Make Payroll Sustainable

Pricing Must Include “Reliability Costs”

  • Caregiver salary / shift wages
  • Supervisor layer + audits
  • Verification + training cost amortization
  • Bench/floaters buffer
  • Customer success + support
  • Tech/CRM + communication costs
  • Margin (so you can scale)

Contribution Margin Framework (Simple)

Contribution Margin per Client (Monthly) =
  Revenue from Client
- Direct caregiver cost allocated
- Supervisor & audit allocation
- Replacement/bench allocation
- Variable ops costs (calls/whatsapp/transport)
= Contribution (₹)
Contribution % = Contribution / Revenue
Target gross margin guidance:
  • Elder care: 35–55% (depends on skill + hours)
  • Baby care: 30–50% (newborn can be premium)
Common trap: underpricing replacement readiness. If you promise reliability, you must price the bench into the package.

10) Trust & Safety: Your Brand Moat

Minimum Safety Standard (Before Deployment)

  • Police verification + identity validation
  • Address verification (caregiver)
  • Reference checks
  • Basic medical fitness check (where feasible)
  • Strict policy for baby care: no phone while holding baby, hygiene compliance
  • Clear written scope + boundaries + escalation
Zero ambiguity policy: If scope is unclear, disputes follow. Every client must have a signed/acknowledged scope document.

12) 90-Day Launch Plan (Pilot → Scale)

Days 1–15: Foundation

  • Finalize packages + SLAs + boundaries documents
  • Create SOPs + care plan + daily logs + incident templates
  • Hire: Trainer/Quality Lead + Scheduler + 1 Supervisor
  • Start caregiver sourcing funnel + verification process

Days 16–45: Pilot (20–30 active clients)

  • Train and certify first 30–50 caregivers
  • Operate in 1–2 tight zones (reduce travel chaos)
  • Run daily attendance and weekly audits
  • Track: fulfillment, replacement SLA, complaints, NPS

Days 46–90: Scale to 100 active clients

  • Add supervisors as per ratio
  • Build floaters bench (10–15%)
  • Launch partnerships with clinics/communities
  • Install renewal cadence + referral engine
Scale rule: Increase zones only when you can maintain the same fulfillment %, audit cadence, and replacement SLA.

Appendix: Quick Policies (Copy into your handbook)

Policy 1 — Phone Usage (Baby Care)
  • No phone usage while holding/feeding/rocking baby
  • Phone allowed only during designated break times
  • Repeated violation → warning → removal
Policy 2 — Boundaries & Scope
  • Scope is defined in the Care Plan
  • Any scope change requires supervisor approval + updated care plan
  • Misaligned expectations are resolved via CS within 24 hours
Policy 3 — Replacement Rules
  • Replacement SLA depends on package and shift type
  • Floater/bench prioritized for high-risk packages (newborn/night/dementia)
  • Continuity is preferred; replacements should not rotate unnecessarily
